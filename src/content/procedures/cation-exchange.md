## Purpose

Determine the cation exchange capacity (CEC) of the mud, expressed as pounds of bentonite equivalent per barrel (lb/bbl). The Methylene Blue Test (MBT) measures the amount of reactive clay (bentonite, drill solids, and contaminants) present in the system.

## Equipment

- Methylene blue solution (0.01 N standardised)
- 3% hydrogen peroxide (H₂O₂)
- Sulphuric acid, dilute (5 N H₂SO₄)
- Titration burette or syringe
- Stirrer or magnetic stir plate
- Whatman filter paper (or equivalent)
- Glass rod
- 2 mL mud sample

## Procedure

1. Add 2 mL of mud sample to a 250 mL Erlenmeyer flask.
2. Add 15 mL of distilled water, 0.5 mL of 5 N H₂SO₄, and 5 mL of 3% H₂O₂.
3. Heat the mixture gently and boil for 10 minutes to oxidise organic matter; allow to cool.
4. Add 40 mL of distilled water and continue stirring.
5. Using the burette, add 1 mL increments of methylene blue solution to the flask while stirring vigorously.
6. After each addition, place a drop of the suspension on filter paper and observe for a turquoise "halo" ring around the central blue spot.
7. Continue adding methylene blue until the halo persists for at least 2 minutes after stirring.
8. Record the total volume of methylene blue solution added (mL).

## Calculations

- MBT (lb/bbl bentonite equivalent) = mL methylene blue solution × 5

## Notes

- The persistent turquoise halo indicates saturation of all exchangeable cation sites.
- If the halo appears immediately at the first addition, dilute the mud sample before repeating.
- Report MBT value in lb/bbl. Elevated MBT may indicate drill-solids build-up or bentonite contamination.
